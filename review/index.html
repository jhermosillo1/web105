Gone Review

Summary

In the Gone series by Michael Grant all the adults disappear in the blink of an eye. All except for the young people. Anyone over the age of 15, poof, gone. All of a sudden, there are no phones, no internet, no televison. No way to get help. And there's also no way to find out what has happened.

With no adults around, hunger threatens. Bullies rule. A sinister creature lurks. Animals are mutating. Teens themselves are also changing, they are developing new talents. Talents that are unimaginable, dangerous, deadly powers that get stronger as the days pass.

It's a terrying new world. Sides are being chosen, and fight, a war. Town people against the rich kids. The bullies against the weak. Powerful against the powerless. But their time runs out, on their 15th birthday, they disappear just like everyone else.

The Gone series is personally my favorite series. It makes you feel like you are living in the book. One thing I like about this series is that it doesnt just give you the perspective from one person, but from multiple characters. This way it shows you the different point of views.

One of my favorite quotes from this book is "We didn't Make this World we're just the poor fools who are living in it." This quote shows how desperate the kids living in this new world that they call the "FAYS."
